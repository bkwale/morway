# Cold Outreach Email — Agri-Accounting Firms (NL/DE/BE)

**Use case:** First touch to agricultural accounting firms in the Netherlands, Germany and Belgium.

---

## Subject Line Options

1. `Your farming clients' invoices — processed in seconds, not minutes`
2. `[Firm Name] — automating invoice processing for your agricultural clients`
3. `What if your team never had to key in another supplier invoice?`

---

## Email Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm building a tool that automates invoice processing for accounting firms with agricultural clients — and I think it could save your team real time.

For firms managing dozens of farming clients, every month brings a pile of supplier invoices — feed, fertiliser, equipment, vet bills, fuel — each one needing to be opened, categorised, coded to the right account, and posted to your accounting system. Manually.

We built Morway to handle exactly this. When an invoice arrives (PDF, email attachment, or structured e-invoice), Morway does three things:

1. **Parses** the invoice automatically — extracts supplier, line items, amounts
2. **Categorises** each line item using rules your firm sets (feed → 5010, fertiliser → 5020, etc.)
3. **Posts** the bill to your accounting system — no manual data entry, no copy-paste

When the system is confident in its categorisation, invoices post automatically. When something doesn't match, it flags it for your team to review in a clean dashboard. You stay in control — the software handles the repetitive work.

For firms that need Peppol e-invoice reception (with EU mandates rolling out), we offer that as an add-on.

We're currently onboarding a small number of firms to shape the product around real workflows. There's no commitment — I'd just like to understand how you handle invoice processing today and whether automation would save your team meaningful time.

Would you have 20 minutes this week or next for a quick call?

Best,

walt@morway.app

## Notes for Personalisation

- **Netherlands:** Reference Dutch agricultural sector (dairy, horticulture, arable farming). NL is moving toward e-invoicing mandates.
- **Germany:** Reference the January 2025 e-invoicing mandate (receiving). Sending obligations phase in by 2028.
- **Belgium:** Reference the January 2026 B2B e-invoicing mandate — now live. Firms are already dealing with increased volume.
- **Firm-specific:** If the firm lists agricultural clients on their website, reference that directly. "I noticed [Firm] works with dairy farmers in [region]..."
- **Size signal:** Target firms with 10-50 staff. Large enough to feel the volume pain, small enough to not have built their own tooling.
- **Peppol add-on:** Only mention if the firm is in a country with an active or imminent mandate. Don't lead with it.
